### The $f$ function
The **$f$-divergence** {(Csiszar, 1963)} generalizes many similarity measures between probability distributions. Let $P$ and $Q$ be two probability distributions over a space $\Omega$ such that $P$ is absolutely continuous with respect the $Q$. Then for a convex function $f$ such that $f(1)=0$, the $f$-divergence of $P$ from $Q$ is defined as 

$$
\begin{eqnarray}
D_f (P||Q) &=& \int_{\Omega} f(\frac{dP}{dQ}) dQ \\
&=& \sum_{x_i} q(x_i) f(\frac{p(x_i)}{q(x_i)})
\end{eqnarray}
$$

The **function** $f(x)$ is defined as,

$$
 f_{\alpha}(x)  = 
  \begin{cases} 
  x\log(x) - (x-1) & \text{for} \quad \alpha = 1 \\
  -\log(x) + (x-1) & \text{fpr} \quad \alpha = 0 \\
  \frac{(x^{\alpha}-1)-\alpha(x-1)}{\alpha(\alpha-1)} & \text{otherwise} \\
  \end{cases}
$$

The **convex conjugate** of $f(x)$ is defined as $$f^{\ast}(y) = \sup_{x\in \text{dom}f}\{\langle y,x \rangle - f(x)\}$$,

$$
 f_{\alpha}^{\ast}(y)  = 
  \begin{cases} 
  e^y - 1  & \text{for} \quad \alpha = 1 \\
  -\log(1-y) & \text{for} \quad \alpha = 0\text{,} y < 1\\
  \frac{1}{\alpha} (1 + (\alpha-1)y)^{\frac{\alpha}{\alpha-1}} - \frac{1}{\alpha} & \text{} \\
  \end{cases}
$$

The **derivatives** of $$f_{\alpha}^{\ast}(y)$$ are,

$$
 (f_{\alpha}^{\ast})'(y)  = 
  \begin{cases} 
  e^y  & \text{for} \quad \alpha = 1 \\
  \frac{1}{1-y} & \text{for} \quad \alpha = 0\text{,} y < 1\\
  \sqrt[\alpha-1]{1+(\alpha-1)y} & \text{for} \quad y(1-\alpha) < 1 \\
  \end{cases}
$$

### The dual function 

The $f$-Divergence constrained policy optimization for ergodi MDPs are solved by minmizing the the dual function,

$$
\begin{eqnarray}
&\min_{\boldsymbol{v}, \lambda, \kappa}& \quad  g(\boldsymbol{v}, \lambda, \kappa) = \eta \sum_{s^{[i]},a^{[i]}} \frac{1}{N} f^{\ast} (\frac{A_v(s^{[i]},a^{[i]})-\lambda + \kappa(s^{[i]}, a^{[i]})}{\eta}) + \lambda\\

&\textrm{s.t.:}& \quad  \kappa(s,a) \geq 0  \quad \forall s \in S, \forall a \in A \\ 
&& \quad \text{arg} f^{\ast} \in \text{range}_{x \geq 0} f'(x), \quad \forall s \in S, \forall a \in A 
\end{eqnarray}
$$


Solutions for $$f$$-divergence: 

$$
\begin{eqnarray}
\nabla_{\boldsymbol{v}} g(\boldsymbol{v}, \lambda, \kappa) &=& 
   \frac{1}{N}  \sum_{s^{[i]},a^{[i]}}\{\mathbb{E}[\varphi(s')] - \varphi(s)\} *  \sqrt[\alpha-1]{1+(\alpha-1) * (A_v(s^{[i]},a^{[i]})-\lambda + \kappa(s^{[i]}, a^{[i]})/\eta)} \\
\nabla_{\lambda} g(\boldsymbol{v}, \lambda, \kappa) &=& \frac{\eta}{N} \sum_{s^{[i]},a^{[i]}}\sqrt[\alpha-1]{1+(\alpha-1) * (A_v(s^{[i]},a^{[i]})-\lambda + \kappa(s^{[i]}, a^{[i]})/\eta)} + 1\\
\nabla_{\kappa} g(\boldsymbol{v}, \lambda, \kappa) && \frac{\eta}{N} \sum_{s^{[i]},a^{[i]}}\sqrt[\alpha-1]{1+(\alpha-1) * (A_v(s^{[i]},a^{[i]})-\lambda + \kappa(s^{[i]}, a^{[i]})/\eta)} \\
\end{eqnarray}
$$

As, comparision, we can also write down the dual function for REPS,

$$
\begin{eqnarray}
&\min_{\boldsymbol{v}}& \quad  g(\boldsymbol{v}) = \eta \log \sum_{\boldsymbol{s^{[i]}, a^{[i]}}} \frac{1}{N} \exp{(\frac{A_{\boldsymbol{v}} (\boldsymbol{s^{[i]}, a^{[i]}})}{\eta}+ \epsilon) } \\

&\textrm{s.t.:}& \quad \eta > 0 \\ 
\end{eqnarray}
$$

Solution for REPS:

$$
\begin{eqnarray}
\nabla_{\boldsymbol{v}} g(\eta, \boldsymbol{v}) = \frac{\sum_i Z_i (\mathbb{E_{p(\boldsymbol{x'|x^{[i]},u^{[i]}})}} [\varphi(\boldsymbol{x}')] - \varphi(\boldsymbol{x}^{[i]})) }{\sum_i Z_i} \quad \text{with} \quad Z_i = \exp{(\frac{\delta_{\boldsymbol{v}} (\boldsymbol{x^{[i]}, u^{[i]}}) - \max \delta_v }{\eta})}
\end{eqnarray}
$$

### Appendix: Derivatives of dual function optimization

**========== Derivative for $\boldsymbol{v}$ ==========**

$$
\begin{eqnarray}
\nabla_{\boldsymbol{v}} g(\boldsymbol{v}, \lambda, \kappa) &=& \nabla_{\boldsymbol{v}} \frac{\eta}{N}  \sum_{s^{[i]},a^{[i]}} f^{\ast} (\frac{A_v(s^{[i]},a^{[i]})-\lambda + \kappa(s^{[i]}, a^{[i]})}{\eta}) + \lambda\\

&=& \frac{\eta}{N}  \sum_{s^{[i]},a^{[i]}}  \nabla_{\boldsymbol{v}}  f_{\alpha}^{\ast} (\frac{A_v(s,a)-\lambda + \kappa(s, a)}{\eta})\\
&=& \frac{\eta}{N}  \sum_{s^{[i]},a^{[i]}} (f_{\alpha}^{\ast})'(y) \nabla_{\boldsymbol{v}}(\frac{A_v(s,a)-\lambda + \kappa(s, a)}{\eta})\\
&=& \frac{\eta}{N}  \sum_{s^{[i]},a^{[i]}} (f_{\alpha}^{\ast})'(y) \frac{\mathbb{E}[\varphi(s')] - \varphi(s)}{\eta}\\
&=& \frac{1}{N}  \sum_{s^{[i]},a^{[i]}}  \{\mathbb{E}[\varphi(s')] - \varphi(s)\}  (f_{\alpha}^{\ast})'(y)\\
\end{eqnarray}
$$


**========== Derivative for $\lambda$ ==========**

$$
\begin{eqnarray}
\nabla_{\lambda} g(\boldsymbol{v}, \lambda, \kappa) &=& \nabla_{\lambda} \frac{\eta}{N}  \sum_{s^{[i]},a^{[i]}} f^{\ast} (\frac{A_v(s^{[i]},a^{[i]})-\lambda + \kappa(s^{[i]}, a^{[i]})}{\eta}) + \lambda\\

&=& \frac{\eta}{N}  \sum_{s^{[i]},a^{[i]}}  \nabla_{\lambda}  f_{\alpha}^{\ast} (\frac{A_v(s,a)-\lambda + \kappa(s, a)}{\eta}) + 1\\
&=& \frac{\eta}{N}  \sum_{s^{[i]},a^{[i]}} (f_{\alpha}^{\ast})'(y) \nabla_{\lambda}(\frac{A_v(s,a)-\lambda + \kappa(s, a)}{\eta}) + 1 \\
&=& \frac{\eta}{N}  \sum_{s^{[i]},a^{[i]}} (f_{\alpha}^{\ast})'(y) + 1\\
\end{eqnarray}
$$


**========== Derivative for $\kappa$ ==========**

$$
\begin{eqnarray}
\nabla_{\kappa} g(\boldsymbol{v}, \lambda, \kappa) &=& \nabla_{\kappa} \frac{\eta}{N}  \sum_{s^{[i]},a^{[i]}} f^{\ast} (\frac{A_v(s^{[i]},a^{[i]})-\lambda + \kappa(s^{[i]}, a^{[i]})}{\eta}) + \lambda\\

&=& \frac{\eta}{N}  \sum_{s^{[i]},a^{[i]}}  \nabla_{\kappa}  f_{\alpha}^{\ast} (\frac{A_v(s,a)-\lambda + \kappa(s, a)}{\eta}) + 0\\
&=& \frac{\eta}{N}  \sum_{s^{[i]},a^{[i]}} (f_{\alpha}^{\ast})'(y) \nabla_{\kappa}(\frac{A_v(s,a)-\lambda + \kappa(s, a)}{\eta}) \\
&=& \frac{\eta}{N}  \sum_{s^{[i]},a^{[i]}} (f_{\alpha}^{\ast})'(y)\\
\end{eqnarray}
$$
