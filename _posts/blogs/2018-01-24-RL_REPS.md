Stochastic gradient descent is commanly used in many machine learning algorithms due to the fact that SGD is straightforward to implement, intuitive to understand and computationally cheap. Though several advanced SGD algorithms that use momentum or adaptive moment have been widely adapted by the machine learning community, second-order methods, e.g., Newton, Gauss-Newton, are still among top interests of the research scientists. In this blog, we talk about a popular policy gradient method---natural policy gradient---and cover several variation of the natural gradient in Reinforcement Learning.


##### **Natural gradient**

> When a parameter space has a certain underlying structure, the ordinary
gradient of a function does not represent its steepest direction, but the
natural gradient does.
