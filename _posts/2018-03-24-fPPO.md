### Policy Optimization

**1. Policy Gradient Methods**

Tranditional policy gradient methods work by computing an estimation of the policy gradient and use stochastic graidient ascent method to update the policy parameters. The objective function commanly used in policy gradient method is 

$$
\begin{eqnarray}
J^{PG}(\theta) = \mathbb{E}[\log \pi_{\theta}(a_t \mid s_t) A_t]
\end{eqnarray}
$$

where $\pi_{\theta}$ is parameterized stochastic policy to be optimized and $A_t$ is estimated advantage function at time step $t$.

**2. Trust Region Methods**

In TRPO, we maximize an objective function <the "surrogate" objective> and constraint the size of the policy update by KL divergence,

$$
\begin{eqnarray}
& \text{maximize}_{\theta} \quad \mathbb{E}[\frac{\pi_{\theta}(a_t \mid s_t)}{\pi_{\theta_{old}} (a_t \mid s_t)} A_t] \\
& \text{subject to} \quad \mathbb{E}[D_{KL}[\pi_{\theta_{old}}, \pi_{\theta}]] \leq \sigma \\
\end{eqnarray}
$$

The contraint optimization problem can efficiently be solved using conjugate gradient algorithm, by making a ***linear apporximation to the objective function and a quadratic approximation to the KL divergence constraint.***

Instead of considering the TRPO problem as constraint optimization problem, it is easier to consider it as a general policy gradient problem with KL divergence as penality to policy update.
The objective function can be expressed as, 

$$
\begin{eqnarray}
J^{KL} = \mathbb{E}[\frac{\pi_{\theta}(a_t \mid s_t)}{\pi_{\theta_{old}} (a_t \mid s_t)} A_t - \beta D_{KL}[\pi_{\theta_{old}}(\cdot \mid s_t), \pi_\theta(\cdot \mid s_t)]]
\end{eqnarray}
$$
 
where $\beta \in \mathbb{R}$ is a hyperparamter to be choose.


**3. Proximal Policy Optimization**

TRPO maximizes a 'surrogate' objective function and constraints the update steps using KL divergence. PPO maximizes also a 'surrogate' function with penalty to the policy that move $r_t(\theta)$ away from 1.
The proposed objective function is 

$$
\begin{eqnarray}
J^{CLIP}(\theta) = \mathbb{E} [\min ( r_t(\theta), \text{clip}(r_t(\theta), 1-\epsilon, 1+\epsilon) A_t)]
\end{eqnarray}
$$

### f-divergence Penalized Policy Optimization

Similar to **TRPO** with KL divergence as penality, we propose a novel method that maximize the same "surrogate" objective function, however with f-divergence as penality. Hence, the objective function is 

$$
\begin{eqnarray}
J^{f} = \mathbb{E}[\frac{\pi_{\theta}(a_t \mid s_t)}{\pi_{\theta_{old}} (a_t \mid s_t)} A_t - \beta D_{f}[\pi_{\theta_{old}}(\cdot \mid s_t), \pi_\theta(\cdot \mid s_t)]]
\end{eqnarray}
$$

where

$$
\begin{eqnarray}
D_f (\pi_{\theta_{old}}(\cdot \mid s_t), \pi_\theta(\cdot \mid s_t)]) = \sum_{t}  \pi_\theta(\cdot \mid s_t) f(\frac{\pi_{\theta_{old}}(\cdot \mid s_t)}{\pi_\theta(\cdot \mid s_t)})
\end{eqnarray}
$$

The **function** $f(x)$ is defined as,

$$
 f_{\alpha}(x)  = 
  \begin{cases} 
  x\log(x) - (x-1) & \text{for} \quad \alpha = 1 \\
  -\log(x) + (x-1) & \text{fpr} \quad \alpha = 0 \\
  \frac{(x^{\alpha}-1)-\alpha(x-1)}{\alpha(\alpha-1)} & \text{otherwise} \\
  \end{cases}
$$

### Policy Optimization with Mixed f-divergence as penalty

TRPO with f-divergence penalty does not perform well as TRPO with KL penalty, or PPO, because when 

$$\beta * D_f(P || Q) \approx \beta * D_{KL}(P || Q) \quad \text{for} \quad \beta \rightarrow 0$$
