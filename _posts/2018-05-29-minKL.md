### KL divergence between two Gaussians

Define two univariate Gaussian distributions $p \sim \mathcal{N}(\mu_1, \sigma_1)$ and $q \sim \mathcal{N}(\mu_2, \sigma_2)$,

$$
p = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi\sigma_1^2}} \exp{\left(-\frac{(x-\mu_1)^2}{2 \sigma_1^2} \right)}  \quad
q = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi\sigma_2^2}} \exp{\left(-\frac{(x-\mu_2)^2}{2 \sigma_2^2}\right)} 
$$

The KL divergence between two (Gaussian) distributions can be defined as following,

$$
\begin{align}
D_{KL}(p, q) &= \int p(x) \log \frac{p(x)}{q(x)} dx \\
& = \int p(x) \log{ \frac{\frac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi\sigma_1^2}} \exp{(-\frac{(x-\mu_1)^2}{2 \sigma_1^2})}  }{\frac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi\sigma_2^2}} \exp{(-\frac{(x-\mu_2)^2}{2 \sigma_2^2})} } }  dx \\
& = \int p(x) (\log{ \frac{\sqrt{2\pi\sigma_2^2}}{\sqrt{2\pi\sigma_1^2}} } + \log{ \exp{(-\frac{(x-\mu_1)^2}{2 \sigma_1^2})} } - \log{ \exp{(-\frac{(x-\mu_2)^2}{2 \sigma_2^2})} }) dx\\
& = \int p(x)\log{(\frac{\sigma_2}{\sigma_1})^{1/2}} dx + \int p(x) \left[ -\frac{(x-\mu_1)^2}{2\sigma_1^2} + \frac{(x-\mu_2)^2}{2\sigma_2^2} \right] dx \\
& = \frac{1}{2} \log{\frac{\sigma_2^2}{\sigma_1^2}} \cdot 1 + \frac{1}{2\sigma_1^2}\left[- \int (x-\mu_1)^2 p(x) dx \right] + \frac{1}{2\sigma_2^2} \int (x-\mu_2)^2 p(x) dx \\
& = \frac{1}{2} \log{\frac{\sigma_2^2}{\sigma_1^2}} - \frac{1}{2 \sigma_1^2} \sigma_1^2 + \frac{1}{2\sigma_2^2} \left[ \int (x - \mu_1 + \mu_1 - \mu_2)^2 p(x)dx \right] \\
& = \frac{1}{2} \log{\frac{\sigma_2^2}{\sigma_1^2}}- \frac{1}{2} +  \\
& \quad \frac{1}{2\sigma_2^2} \left( \int (x-\mu_1)^2 p(x)dx + (\mu_1 - \mu_2)^2\int p(x)dx + 2(\mu_1 - \mu_2) \int (x - \mu_1)p(x)dx \right)\\
& = \frac{1}{2} \log{\frac{\sigma_2^2}{\sigma_1^2}}- \frac{1}{2} +  \frac{1}{2\sigma_2^2} \left( \sigma_1^2 + (\mu_1 - \mu_2)^2 + 0 \right) \\
& =  \log{\frac{\sigma_2}{\sigma_1}} + \frac{\sigma_1^2 + (\mu_1 - \mu_2)^2}{2\sigma_2^2} - \frac{1}{2}
\end{align}  
$$

KL divergence is also the *relative entropy*, 

$$
\begin{align}
D_{KL}(p, q) &= \int p(x) \log \frac{p(x)}{q(x)} dx \\
&= -\int p(x) \log{q(x)} dx + \int p(x) \log{p(x)} dx \\
&=  H(p, q) - H(p)
\end{align}
$$

where $H(p, q)$ is the [cross entropy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cross_entropy) of $p$ and $q$, and $H(p)$ is the [entropy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Entropy_(information_theory)) of $p$.

### Minimize KL divergence between policy distributions
Suppose the policy in our reinforcement learning problem is a Gaussian distribution $\pi(a| \mu, \sigma) \sim \mathcal{N}(a| \mu_{\theta}(s), \sigma)$, where $\theta$ are trainable parameters of a deep neural network [deep NN], and $\sigma$ are trainable variables. The output of the deep NN is the mean of the Gaussian distribution. During policy update, the KL divergence between new policy and old policy is, 

$$
\begin{align}
D_{\text{KL}}(\pi_{\theta}, \pi_{\theta_{\text{old}}}) & =  \log{\frac{\sigma_\text{old}}{\sigma}} + \frac{\sigma^2 + (\mu_{\theta} - \mu_{\theta_{\text{old}}})^2}{2\sigma_\text{old}^2} - \frac{1}{2},\\
& \text{where} \quad \sigma_\text{old}, \mu_{\theta_{\text{old}}} \quad  \text{are constants}\\
\end{align}
$$

**1. Compute the gradient with respect to the parameters $\theta$ of the deep NN,**

$$
\begin{align}
\frac{\partial }{\partial \theta} D_{\text{KL}}(\pi_{\theta}, \pi_{\theta_{\text{old}}}) & = \frac{\partial }{\partial \theta} \left( \log{\frac{\sigma_\text{old}}{\sigma}} + \frac{\sigma^2 + (\mu_{\theta} - \mu_{\theta_{\text{old}}})^2}{2\sigma_\text{old}^2} - \frac{1}{2} \right) \\
&= 0 +  \frac{\partial }{\partial \theta}  \left( \frac{\sigma^2 + (\mu_{\theta} - \mu_{\theta_{\text{old}}})^2}{2\sigma_\text{old}^2} \right )- 0 \\
&= \frac{1}{2\sigma_\text{old}^2} \frac{\partial }{\partial \theta}(\mu_{\theta} - \mu_{\theta_{\text{old}}})^2  \\
\end{align}
$$

Minimizing the KL divergence with respect to the parameters $\theta$ of the policy network is equivalent to minimize the **Mean Square Error [MSE]** bewteen the mean of old policy $\pi_{\theta_\text{old}} \sim \mathcal{N}(\mu_{\theta_\text{old}}, \sigma_\text{old})$ and the mean of new policy $\pi_{\theta}  \sim \mathcal{N}(\mu_\theta, \sigma)$.

$$
\frac{\partial }{\partial \theta} D_{\text{KL}}(\pi_{\theta}, \pi_{\theta_{\text{old}}})  \propto  \frac{\partial }{\partial \theta}(\mu_{\theta} - \mu_{\theta_{\text{old}}})^2 
$$

In other words, we treat it as a supervised learning problem, where states $s$ are the input variables, $\mu_{\theta_\text{old}}(s)$ are the target labels and $y = \mu_{\theta}(s)$ are the predicted labels. 

**2. Compute the gradient with respect to the variables $\sigma$, which are state-independent variances of the policy distributions.**

$$
\begin{align}
\frac{\partial }{\partial \sigma} D_{\text{KL}}(\pi_{\theta}, \pi_{\theta_{\text{old}}}) & = \frac{\partial }{\partial \sigma} \left( \log{\frac{\sigma_\text{old}}{\sigma}} + \frac{\sigma^2 + (\mu_{\theta} - \mu_{\theta_{\text{old}}})^2}{2\sigma_\text{old}^2} - \frac{1}{2} \right) \\
&= \frac{\partial }{\partial \sigma} \left( \log{\frac{\sigma_\text{old}}{\sigma}} \right) +  \frac{\partial }{\partial \sigma} \left( \frac{\sigma^2 + (\mu_{\theta} - \mu_{\theta_{\text{old}}})^2}{2\sigma_\text{old}^2} \right )- 0 \\
&= \frac{\sigma}{\sigma_\text{old} \ln(10)} \left( - \frac{\sigma_\text{old}}{\sigma^2} \right) + \frac{\sigma}{\sigma_\text{old}^2} \\
&=  - \frac{1}{\sigma \ln(10)} + \frac{\sigma}{\sigma_\text{old}^2}  = 0\\
\Rightarrow \frac{1}{\sigma \ln(10)} &= \frac{\sigma}{\sigma_\text{old}^2} \\
\sigma^2 \ln(10) &= \sigma_\text{old}^2  \\
\sigma &\approx \sigma_\text{old}
\end{align}
$$

Minimizing KL divergence with respect to the variance $\sigma$ of Gaussian distributions leads to $\sigma \approx \sigma_\text{old}$.

### Minimize KL divergence with ramp function mapping

In my master thesis, I proposed a novel minmax formulation obtained by applying a ramp-transformation to the standard KL divergence constraint, 

$$
\min_{\theta} L_\text{KL}(\pi_{\theta}) = \max{ \left( \mathbb{ \hat{E}}\left[  D_\text{KL}(\pi_{\theta}(\cdot | s_t) \| \pi_{\theta_\text{old}}(\cdot | s_t) ) \right] - \epsilon, 0 \right)} 
$$

**1. $\mathbb{\hat{E}} \left[ D_\text{KL}(\pi_{\theta}(\cdot \mid s_t) \| \pi_{\theta_\text{old}}(\cdot \mid s_t) ) \right] \leq \epsilon$**

$$
\begin{align}
\frac{\partial}{\partial \theta} L_\text{KL}(\pi_{\theta}) &= \frac{\partial}{\partial \theta} 0\\
& = 0
\end{align}
$$

We do not penalize the update step when the information loss satisfies the KL bound. 

**2. $\mathbb{\hat{E}} \left[ D_\text{KL}(\pi_{\theta}(\cdot \mid s_t) \| \pi_{\theta_\text{old}}(\cdot \mid s_t) ) \right] > \epsilon$**

$$
\begin{align}
\frac{\partial}{\partial \theta} L_\text{KL}(\pi_{\theta}) &= \frac{\partial}{\partial \theta}  \left(  \mathbb{ \hat{E}}\left[  D_\text{KL}(\pi_{\theta}(\cdot | s_t) \| \pi_{\theta_\text{old}}(\cdot | s_t) ) \right] - \epsilon  \right)\\
&= \frac{\partial}{\partial \theta}  \left(  \mathbb{ \hat{E}}\left[  D_\text{KL}(\pi_{\theta}(\cdot | s_t) \| \pi_{\theta_\text{old}}(\cdot | s_t) ) \right]  \right) -  0 \\
&= \mathbb{ \hat{E}}\left[ \frac{\partial}{\partial \theta}  D_\text{KL}(\pi_{\theta}(\cdot | s_t) \| \pi_{\theta_\text{old}}(\cdot | s_t) ) \right] \\
\end{align}
$$

The update step are penalized when the information loss violates the KL bound. 

### KL divergence is also called relative entropy

$$
\begin{align}
D_{\text{KL}}(\pi_{\theta}, \pi_{\theta_{\text{old}}}) &= \int \pi_{\theta}  \log \frac{\pi_{\theta}}{\pi_{\theta_{\text{old}}} } dx \\
&= H(\pi_{\theta}, \pi_{\theta_{\text{old}}}) - H(\pi_{\theta}) \\
\end{align}
$$
